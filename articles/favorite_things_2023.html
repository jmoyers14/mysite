My Favorite Things 2023

I compiled this list of my favorite things as way to reflect on the year 2023. There's no overarching theme; each item has impacted me in one way or another, and I wish to share and preserve these memories as my way of remembering the year. This was inspired by my friend Rick's article from 2022.

Day: October 20, 2023
The best day of my life thus far – the day I married my beautiful, kind, and hilarious wife, Nona. It was a culmination of many wonderful moments: the relief of concluding our wedding planning, the warmth of love from our friends and family, and the excitement of embarking on a new chapter in my life.

YouTube: Caleb Hammer's Financial Audit
Nona and I are drawn to Financial Audits the same way passing drivers are captivated by a car wreck. For each guest, Caleb reviews one month of their personal finanical statements and helps them build a budget to payoff their dept or meet whatever financial goal they may have. Caleb navigates each session skillfully with enthusiasm and empathy, while compelling the guests to confront the realities of their financial situations. While Nona and I are not in as dire straits as many of Caleb's guests, the series has helped us to examine our own spending and develop better financial plans so we don't "end up dying on the walmart floor".

Beach: Ocean Beach
Living just two miles from the coast, visiting Ocean Beach has become an part of my morning routine. Every morning, I load Bill and my surfboard into my 4Runner and head to the beach. After a short but sometimes relaxplaying and occasionally terrifying surf session, I take Bill to play with his friends at the dog beach. I've compiled a video featuring about a second from every visit we made to Ocean Beach in 2023.

Podcast: Matt & Shane’s Secret Podcast
With some 4 million podcasts in existance most podcasters have little hope of ever breaking into the top 100, especially if the schtick is “we’re comedians talking about stuff”. Yet something about Matt and Shanes Secret Podcast resonated enough to reach the #1 spot on Patreon. Shane and co-host Matt McCusker offers a new persptive in a comedy landscape largely influenced by Los Angeles and New York comedians. Regularly featuring guests from the Philadelphia comedy scene, it's fun to watch new comedians rise to prominence, similar to how many comedians gained fame from Joe Rogan's podcast.

Place: Maui
We visited Maui just two months before the island would withstand 'the worst natural disaster' in the history of Hawaii. A series of wind-driven wildfires led to widespread damage and killed at least 100 people. We stayed with our friends Hayden and Ana at their condo in Māʻalaea Bay, north of Kihei and south of Lahaina. I still have a hard time believing the town of Lahaina is completely destroyed.

Despite the impending disaster, our time in Maui was filled with unforgettable moments. We hiked to waterfalls, snorkeled with turtles, and enjoyed some amazing sunsets. Surfing here was a bit different compared to California. The water was cleaner and warmer, but the waves broke mostly over shallow reef. A local taught me the importance of 'falling flat' to avoid getting scraped up by the reef, a precaution not typically needed at a beach break in San Diego. We eagerly look forward to returning to Maui once it has recovered.

Brewery: pFriem
During the two years I've been with TrovaTrip, I've had the opportunity to visit Portland, Oregon eight times. Portland excels in craft beer, and whenever I'm in town, I make sure to a West Coast-style IPA from pFriem. Amidst the rising popularity of hazy IPAs, it's refreshing to find breweries that still brew IPAs that just blast your face off with hops. Should you come across their beers on tap (and you like that sorta thing), I highly recommend giving it a try.

Album: One More Time by Blink 182
Blink-182 has been my favorite band since I first statred discovering music. I’ve always felt a connection to Blink-182 becuase they originated in my home town of Poway. So when I heard that they were releasing their first album in eight years with all three original members on my wedding day, it felt somewhat natural. As an album, One More Time really delivers. With its massive-sounding power chords, upbeat melodies, teenage angst and sophomoric humor, it's everything you could want from a Blink album.

Technology: Chat GPT
It's hard for me to imagine life before Google Search, and I believe that in 10 years, it'll be just as challenging to remember life before GPTs. It’s pretty wild to watch a computer program write code based off a description I gave it. Or even better, watch a computer program write a description based off code I give it. ChatGPT is a great tool for rapidly prototyping various ideas. Regular requests like 'Write tests for this', 'Document this', or 'Explain this configuration file’ are common in my workflow. It's a mix of awe and apprehension to see the direction technology is taking.
