My Favorite Things 2023

For my first post I want to reflect on the 2023 by compiling a list of my favorite things. There’s no particular theme; They are just things I like that I want to remember and share. This is inspired by my friend Rick’s fantastic article from 2022. I hopy you write another :)

Day: October 20, 2023
The best day of my life thus far – the day I married my beautiful, kind, and hilarious wife, Nona. It was a culmination of many wonderful moments: the relief of concluding our wedding planning, the warmth of love from our friends and family, and the excitement of embarking on a new chapter in my life.

YouTube: Caleb Hammer's Financial Audit
Nona and I are drawn to Financial Audits in the same way drivers are captivated by a car wreck on the freeway. For each guest Caleb reviews one month of their personal finanical statements and helps them build a budget to payoff their dept or meet whatever financial goal they may have. Caleb navigates each session skillfully with enthusiasm and empathy, while compelling the guests to confront the realities of their financial situations. While Nona and I are not in as dire straits as many of Caleb's guests, the series has helped us to examine our own spending and develop better financial plans and habits heading into 2024.

Beach: Ocean Beach
Living just two miles from the coast, visiting Ocean Beach has become an part of my morning routine. Every morning, I load Bill and my surfboard into my 4Runner and head to the beach. After a short but sometimes relaxplaying and occasionally terrifying surf session, I take Bill to play with his friends at the dog beach. I've compiled a video featuring about a second from every visit we made to Ocean Beach in 2023.

Podcast: Matt & Shane’s Secret Podcast
Listeners of the Joe Rogan Podcast are probably familiar with Shane Gillis’s explosion in popularity throughout 2020s. You might think there enough room in the podcast space for another podcast where comedians sit around and talk but “Matt & Shane’s Secrect Podcast” grew to become the #1 podcast on Patreon in 2023. Shane, along with co-host Matt McCusker offers a fresh persptive in a comedy landscape largely influenced by Los Angeles and New York comedians. Regularly featuring guests from the Philadelphia comedy circuit, it's exciting to see which of these new comedians will rise to prominence through MSSP, similar to the way many have gained fame from appearances on Joe Rogan's podcast.

Brewery: pFriem
During the two years I've been with TrovaTrip, I've had the opportunity to visit Portland, Oregon eight times. Portland excels in craft beer, and whenever I'm in town, I make sure to enjoy some of the West Coast-style IPAs from pFriem. Amidst the rising popularity of hazy IPAs, it's refreshing to find breweries that still deliver beers that blast your face off with hops like pFriem. Should you come across their beers on tap, I highly recommend giving them a try.
